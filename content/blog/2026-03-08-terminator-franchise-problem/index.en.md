I finished watching “Terminator: Zero”. A typical story. Overall, it’s not very different from all the films that have been released, except for the fourth one. And maybe the 2008 TV series had something different, but I haven’t seen it.

By the way, there’s a funny fact about the films.

Terminator 4 was planned as the start of a new trilogy, but it didn’t meet box office expectations, so the trilogy was canceled.

Terminator 5 ignores the events of parts 3 and 4. It was also planned as the start of a new trilogy, but it didn’t meet box office expectations, so the trilogy was canceled.

Terminator 6 ignores the events of parts 3, 4, and 5. It too was planned as the start of a new trilogy, but it didn’t meet box office expectations, so the trilogy was canceled.

It’s a pity that the fourth film never got a continuation — it actually had some real world-building (or maybe it just stuck in my memory well; I only watched it once 15 years ago). Otherwise, the creators seem to have a strong desire for viewers to come watch the same movie they saw last time. Ideally, of course, they’d probably prefer not to shoot a new film at all so they wouldn’t have to spend money…

The lack of development is one of the problems of the whole series. From film to film there is a bad future. From that future someone is sent back to kill or save someone. Chases and shootouts follow, the right person gets saved in the end, and that’s it. The formula worked twice.

Another example of a world without development is “Jurassic Park / Jurassic World.” The first film in the series makes an impressive amount of money, and then the box office steadily declines from film to film (even though the numbers are still quite high). I can’t really imagine what “world development” would look like in this case, but as a spectacle film it’s amazing. Maybe making a continuous series isn’t the best idea here, but revisiting that world once every 5–10 years and making roughly the same kind of movie could work perfectly well.

As the box office shows, people don’t love Terminators as much as they love dinosaurs — not enough to keep making almost the same movie over and over and still make a profit.

What could world development look like? For example, you could show the future world like it was in the fourth film. You could also make the world more realistic — show what AI actually looks like, how it works, and how people relate to it today. There are supporters and opponents — that’s already a conflict. Does humanity really have to be destroyed? Maybe the world will start producing humanoid robots that gradually enter everyday life and begin replacing people. You could also add the uncanny valley effect, when people can’t tell whether they’re looking at a robot or a human.

Another problem is time travel. I wrote about it yesterday. They’ve played with it so much that it no longer affects anything. Time travel either doesn’t affect the present from which the person/Terminator was sent, or the story can always be rewritten and any sacrifices mean nothing. And the story itself ends up meaning nothing, because today you have one chain of events, tomorrow you can forget it and create a new one. And that’s already exactly what’s happening.

And of course — the indestructible Terminators… Although they’re only “indestructible” when they’re fighting on the side of the bad guys. If they’re on your side, they suddenly start aging, malfunctioning, and generally aren’t as cool anymore. An entire arsenal gets fired into a Terminator and it doesn’t care at all. I understand that Terminators come from the “distant” future (just a few decades away!), but weapons against them should work (though then the movie probably wouldn’t exist, but that’s another question).

The world of Terminator is interesting, but it lacks development — or perhaps it needs a complete rethinking and a rejection of the old characters around whom everything currently revolves.
